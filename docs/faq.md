# Frequently Asked Questions

---

### Is EVE a platform?

No.

EVE is a framework and language for recognizing value beyond transactions.
It does not host users, manage data, or run applications.

---

### Is EVE a protocol or blockchain project?

No.

EVE does not require:
- tokens
- blockchains
- smart contracts
- decentralized identity

Those may be used, but are optional.

---

### Is EVE anti-capitalist or ideological?

No.

EVE is neutral by design.
It can be used by:
- public institutions
- private companies
- non-profits
- community initiatives

EVE does not prescribe outcomes â€” it makes value visible.

---

### Is this a reputation or scoring system?

No.

EVE avoids:
- permanent scores
- global rankings
- identity-bound value histories

SATE signals are contextual and time-bound.

---

### How is this different from ESG or KPIs?

EVE complements existing metrics.

It focuses on:
- participation
- attention
- time
- lived contribution

Where traditional metrics focus on outcomes, EVE makes contribution legible.

---

### Do I need to use incentives?

No.

Incentives are optional.
Many implementations work at:
- recognition level
- social rewards
- utility rewards

Convertible rewards should be used carefully.

---

### How does EVE prevent gaming?

Through:
- proportional incentives
- lightweight verification
- pattern-based design
- avoidance of permanent scoring

EVE assumes edge cases and designs for them early.

---

### Who governs EVE?

EVE is stewarded, not controlled.

Governance focuses on:
- framework integrity
- ethical guardrails
- interoperability

Implementations govern themselves.

---

### Can EVE integrate with existing systems?

Yes.

EVE is designed to sit beneath systems as a value layer.
It integrates via adapters, not mandates.

---

### Is EVE finished?

No.

EVE is intentionally incomplete.
It evolves through:
- use
- feedback
- real-world application

Stability matters more than novelty.
